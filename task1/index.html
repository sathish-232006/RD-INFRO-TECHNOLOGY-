Biography

Ratan Tata is an Indian industrialist and former chairman of Tata Group. Born on December 28, 1937, he led the group's global expansion, including acquiring Jaguar Land Rover. Known for his philanthropy and visionary leadership, Tata remains a respected figure in business and social sectors across India and beyond. Educated at Cornell University and Harvard Business School, Tata joined the Tata Group in 1962. He became chairman in 1991, ushering in a new era of innovation and global ambition. Under his leadership, Tata Motors introduced the Tata Nano, the world’s cheapest car, aiming to make transportation affordable. He is also known for his strong ethical values and commitment to nation-building. After retiring in 2012, he continues to guide the group as Chairman Emeritus and actively supports startups and social causes through Tata Trusts. Ratan Tata has received numerous honors, including the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan.
